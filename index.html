Streetlight Data  
Angular
Node

Streetlights are at the front lines of the smart city. They offer a public place to hang cameras, sensors, wireless antennas and more. They also provide new revenue streams by renting out space to hang things or lowering energy costs through LED conversions. But how do policy makers decide how to use this newly valuable asset, and how do residents weigh in on the process? We want to make information about how are streetlights are used more transparent and accessible.

Free the Lots  
Laravel
PHP

Kansas City’s vacant lots are a constant issue our coders are trying to fix. A major obstacle to rehabbing abandoned and blighted properties in the urban core of Kansas City are legal claims against properties. “Free the Lots” is going to help potential home buyers identify which properties have extra debt attached to the sale, assisting the city, the county, the sellers and buyers. Neighborhood Legal Support (NLS) are invested in this project and Gregg Lombardi will be championing it! He needs subject matter expertise in clearing titles, also needs software developers, designers.

Code for KC Dashboard  
Web Development
SQL

Code for KC Dashboard will be a web-based tool for the brigade to use internally to serve three main functions. Brigade leadership will be able to collect and track project statuses, helping build brigade capacity and obtain needed resources. Brigade members will be able to search for other members based on skill, so projects can recruit additional team members and request consultations. Automated attendance tracking will save time, and aid marketing and promotion efforts for Hack Nights and events.

KC Tow Lots  
React
Node

KCMO runs monthly auctions of cars and other items (boats, scooters, etc) that are unclaimed from city tow lots, but information about the items up for auction isn’t easily accessible by salvage dealers. A list of the vehicles up for auction is available online that includes limited information about the condition of the vehicles.


There is an opportunity to increase revenue, increase sales, reduce waste (into the landfill), or even connect more people to low cost vehicles with better access to more, relevant data.

ReUseFull  
Web Design
Front End

Many people have excess stuff that could still be put to good use but don't know what organizations might accept it. Nonprofit organizations need to stretch their budgets, but people have to know about the organizations in order to check their website or social media to learn about what types of in-kind donations an organization might need. Nonprofits often get in-kind donations they don't need or have issues with storing large amounts of donations they do need.


Re.Use.Full is a website that connects people with stuff they no longer need with nonprofit organizations that could use it.
